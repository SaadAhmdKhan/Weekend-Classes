Wikipedia:

Wikipedia, often referred to as the "free encyclopedia," is a vast online resource that provides information on a wide range of topics. It is collaboratively edited and maintained by volunteers around the world, making it one of the largest and most comprehensive reference works available. Founded in 2001 by Jimmy Wales and Larry Sanger

Wikipedia has grown to include millions of articles in multiple languages, covering subjects such as history, science, technology, culture, and more. While its open-editing model has been subject to criticism for potential inaccuracies and biases, Wikipedia remains a valuable source of information for millions of users worldwide, serving as a starting point for research and exploration on virtually any topic imaginable.
